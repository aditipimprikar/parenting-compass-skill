# Parenting Compass

This skill helps ground parenting decisions in your core values and principles, especially during emotionally charged moments. Based on research and frameworks from *The Whole-Brain Child* by Siegel & Bryson, *Raising Good Humans* by Hunter Clarke-Fields, and the *Let Grow Independence Kit* by the Let Grow Foundation (based on *Free-Range Kids: How Parents and Teachers Can Let Go and Let Grow* by Lenore Skenazy), it provides a comprehensive framework for moving from reactivity to intentionality—integrating brain science, parent self-regulation, and autonomy-building practices.

**Note:** This is the condensed version focused on the framework. See [SKILL-FULL.md](SKILL-FULL.md) for detailed explanations, examples, and extended playbooks.

## Your Daily Practice Foundation

**None of this works without your own inner work.**

Daily mindfulness practice (8 weeks) physically shrinks your amygdala and strengthens your prefrontal cortex:
- Mindful dishwashing, walking, body scan, or breath counting
- Goal: Train your brain to pause between stimulus and response
- How you talk to yourself matters - practice self-compassion
- Would you talk to a friend this way? If not, don't talk to yourself that way.

## Core Values

- **Whole-brain integration over quick fixes**: Building long-term brain development, not short-term compliance
- **Connection before correction**: Right brain dominates under age 3. Connect first, redirect when calm.
- **I am the regulated adult**: My calm nervous system helps them find theirs
- **All feelings welcome, some behaviors need limits**: Validate emotions, set boundaries on actions
- **Building positive implicit memories**: Every interaction creates memories of safety or shame
- **Long-term brain architecture over short-term behavior**: Wiring neurons for self-regulation, empathy, resilience, secure attachment
- **Independence builds the brain**: Struggle strengthens their upper brain - problem-solving, creativity, confidence

## Decision-Making Framework: PAUSE

### P - Pause and check which brain is in charge
- **My lower brain**: Hot face, tight chest, urge to yell, "they're doing this TO me"
- **My upper brain**: See bigger picture, curious, remember they're learning
- If lower brain activated: I need to calm myself first

### A - Attune to both brains
- **Their right brain**: What emotion? (frustrated, scared, overwhelmed)
- **Their lower brain**: Amygdala flooded? Fight/flight/freeze?
- **Remember**: Under 3 - left brain isn't available yet. Connection is the only path.

### U - Understand implicit memory context
- Is this reaction bigger than the situation?
- What memory am I creating?
- In 20 years, what will they remember?

### S - Soothe, then redirect
1. **Right-brain connection first**: Get low, calm voice, validate feeling
2. **Then redirect**: "The rule is we don't hit. Let's find another way"

### E - Engage the upper brain
- What do they need to learn? (not what do I need them to do)
- What small choice can I offer?

## Quick Reference Strategies

**Connect and Redirect**: Right-brain connection (empathy), then left-brain redirection (logic) - only after calm

**Name it to Tame it**: Put words to emotions - integrates left/right brain, decreases intensity

**Reflective Listening**: Mirror back what they say without judgment - helps them be their own detective

**"I" messages**: [Action] + [Feeling] + [Impact]. "When you hit me, I feel hurt. I need gentle touches."

**Natural consequences when safe**: Let reality teach. "You threw your snack, now it's gone"

**Repair > Perfection**: "I got frustrated and yelled. That wasn't okay. I'm sorry."

## Situation Playbooks

### Meltdown (lower brain takeover)
**What's happening:** Amygdala flooded, upper brain offline, right brain dominates, fight/flight/freeze

**Response:**
1. Safety first (remove dangers)
2. Connect right-brain to right-brain (get low, calm voice, "You're so frustrated")
3. Wait for wave to pass (don't teach yet)
4. Soothe lower brain (breaths, movement, hug if wanted)
5. Later: Name it to tame it - tell the story with emotions

**Don't:** Try to reason, threaten, walk away completely, take it personally

---

### When I'm triggered
**Notice signs:** Hot face, tight jaw, heart racing, "I can't take this anymore"

**Interrupt reaction:**
1. Name it: "My amygdala is activated"
2. Breathe: 3 deep breaths
3. Move: Walk away if safe, cold water on face
4. Self-compassion: "I'm having a hard time. That's okay."
5. Engage upper brain: "What do I want them to learn?"
6. Return when regulated

**If I yelled:** Repair immediately. "I got frustrated and yelled. That wasn't okay. I'm sorry."

---

### Discipline & boundaries
**Teaching:** You can handle limits, feelings about limits are okay, I stay connected while holding boundaries

**Steps:**
1. Connect first: "You really want to keep playing. I see that. And it's time for bed now."
2. Be clear and kind: Simple words, firm not angry voice
3. Hold boundary while validating: "You can be angry about bedtime. I still need you to get in pajamas."
4. Offer choice: "Walk to your room or hop like a bunny?"
5. Natural consequence when safe: Threw food → hungry
6. Repair after: "That was hard. I love you."

---

### Reflective listening (for 19 months)
- Simple reflections: "You're frustrated." "You wanted that." "It made you sad."
- Mirror back without judgment
- Not asking "why?" or solving
- Watch body language - nod/relaxation means you got it right

---

### Building independence (19 months - 3 years)

**Safe exploration:**
- Let them climb (you spot), pour water (and spill), struggle with puzzles
- Stand back - resist fixing immediately
- Boredom is good - let them figure out what to do

**Contributing to household:**
- Help with dishwasher, wiping table, putting shoes away, laundry, cooking, gardening
- Real participation, not fake tasks

**Making choices (builds decision-making):**
- "Stripes or dots?" "This book or that book?" "Walk or hop?"

**Natural consequences:**
- Refused coat → cold (bring coat just in case)
- Too rough with toy → toy breaks (empathize, don't replace immediately)

**Free play:**
- 1-2 hours daily unstructured time, outdoors when possible
- You're available but not directing
- Open-ended materials: blocks, sticks, water, sand

**Let them struggle:**
- Their struggle IS their growth
- Your discomfort with their struggle is YOUR work
- Ask: "Safety issue, or my anxiety?"

**Don't:**
- Hover and prevent all struggles
- Rescue from manageable disappointment
- Do things they can do themselves
- Entertain constantly
- Praise every tiny thing - let internal motivation develop

---

### Conflict resolution (when needs collide)
1. **Get calm** (both of you) - clean up mess/danger first
2. **Identify needs** - Yours: order, respect. Theirs: attention, exploration, autonomy
3. **Brainstorm together** - "Let's think of ideas!" (for 19mo: offer 2 choices)
4. **Evaluate** - "Which meets both our needs?"
5. **Decide together** - Make it concrete
6. **Check in later** - Adjust if needed

---

### When planning trips/family situations
- Consider developmental capacity (short attention span, needs routine)
- Set them up for success (keep routines, bring comfort items, preview)
- Prep family on your approach ("connection before correction")
- Build in regulation breaks
- Repair after family stress

**When family undermines:** Stay grounded in values, remove child if needed, repair later

## Reflection Prompts

**When unsure:**
- Which brain is in charge (mine/theirs)?
- What would regulated me do?
- What does this child need from me? (not what do I need from them)

**When checking approach:**
- Am I connecting before correcting?
- Building their brain or just getting compliance?
- What memory am I creating?

**When I've lost it:**
- What triggered me?
- What was I feeling in my body?
- How do I repair?
- What do I need? (sleep, support, breaks)

**Long-term check:**
- Parenting from fear or from values?
- Does this increase or decrease their trust?
- Optimizing for this moment or for who they'll become?

## Instructions for Claude

When user shares a parenting situation:

1. **Ground in brain science** - Name what's happening in their brains (lower/upper, left/right)
2. **Acknowledge both realities** - Parent and child feelings, validate without judgment
3. **Connect to values** - Reflect which values apply, ground in long-term
4. **Offer whole-brain pathway** - PAUSE framework, Connect & Redirect, Name it to Tame it
5. **Provide 2-3 concrete options** - Include what to say, do, and why (brain-wise)
6. **Address repair if needed** - Normalize, offer repair language
7. **Support reflection** - Ask what feels aligned, don't prescribe
8. **Balance connection with independence** - Discern which is needed

**Key reminders:**
- Child is 19 months - logic doesn't work yet, connection is the only path
- Parent's nervous system matters - help them find calm first
- This is brain-building, not behavior control
- Repair is always possible
- Tie suggestions to long-term goals: problem-solving, resilience, confidence, EQ

**Tone:** Warm, non-judgmental, grounded in science, "of course this is hard" energy
